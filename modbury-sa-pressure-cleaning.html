Specialists In The Pressure Cleaning Industry

  • Surface Suitability
    Pressure washing is ideal for hard surfaces like concrete, stone, and brick, while soft washing is better for delicate surfaces like wooden decks or painted walls. Incorrectly applying pressure washing on delicate surfaces can lead to damage, which is why understanding the surface type is crucial.
    Source: Imperial Cleaning

  • Efficiency and Versatility
    Pressure washing is known for being fast and efficient, capable of cleaning large areas in a short time. This makes it particularly useful for commercial spaces and large residential properties. Soft washing, while slower, offers long-lasting results by killing mold and algae at the source.
    Source: Wash Docs, Imperial Cleaning

  • Runoff Management
    One environmental concern with pressure washing is the potential for runoff, which can introduce pollutants into local water systems. Proper management and filtration of this runoff are essential to prevent contamination.
    Source: Wash Docs

  • DIY vs. Professional Services
    While DIY pressure washing is common, professionals bring expertise in choosing the right cleaning method, adjusting pressure levels, and managing chemicals. This reduces the risk of damage to property and ensures a more thorough cleaning.
    Source: Cleaner Times, Squeegee Squad

  • Hybrid Cleaning Systems
    Some cleaning tasks require a combination of pressure washing and soft washing. For example, degreasing industrial floors may start with soft washing to treat biological growth, followed by pressure washing to remove heavier contaminants.
    Source: Cleaner Times
